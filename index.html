reading you

a quick check-in

When a user opens the app, miji will use the user's front camera and begin running its perception engine to read their facial micro-expressions in real time. Subtle shifts in the brow and mouth, micro-movements around the eyes, and other facial landmarks are all mapped against a running emotional baseline unique to each user. The entire process takes just a few moments from launch to computed reflection. No buttons, no prompts, and no interaction required. The result is a single emotional snapshot that serves as the entry point for every other feature in the app.

what it reads

The perception engine continues to track forty-two facial landmarks across six primary expression zones. It measures both the static position of each feature and its rate of change over the reading window. Composite signals like tension gradients and bilateral symmetry are weighted into the final assessment. The system uses aggregate historical assessments to both adapt to each user's sensitivity over time, accounting for natural resting expressions and individual variation. Every perception process runs through a lightweight on-device model with no cloud dependency.

your baseline, not theirs

Every reading is compared against the user's own historical data, not a population average. As a result, miji's readings and scores become more accurate and more personal the longer it gets to learn about the user's life. A slight brow furrow might be unremarkable for one person while indicating a significant shift for another. miji learns those individual distinctions and adapts accordingly. There are no normative benchmarks and no comparisons made against anyone else.

privacy by design

Any raw sensor data processed during a session with miji will never leave the user's device. The perception engine processes the video feed locally, extracts the relevant landmarks, and discards the raw frames immediately. Only derived emotional scores are retained and no user specific biometric identifiers or facial geometry ever leave the phone. Each permission granted can be toggled with standalone toggles. Disabling any permission won't affect active features or enabled permissions.

your score

how it's calculated

Every day you get a single number measured entirely against your own life and history. No population benchmarks, no comparison, no ranking against anyone. miji generates a weighted composite of three distinct pillars: groundedness (how centered versus reactive you are today), connection quality (the strength of your social and emotional interactions), and trajectory (your directional trend over the past week). Each pillar pulls from different parts of the app, from face reads to journal sentiment to your interactions with miji. The weighting adjusts dynamically based on which data streams have the most signal on a given day. If you skip the journal for a week, your score might lean more heavily on face reads and interaction data. The system never penalizes missing inputs; however, both miji's scoring accuracy and interaction quality increase over consistent check-ins and journaling sessions with you.

personal baseline

The score is always relative to your historical range. A 72 for one person might represent a strong recovery week, while the same number for someone else might signal a slow decline. Your baseline recalibrates continuously over a rolling window of the last sixty days. Sudden life changes like a trip, an illness, or something unexpected are weighted differently to avoid distorting your trend score. The goal is a number that feels honest to your circumstances. miji is a personal reflection tool, a daily touchpoint and a long-term record, not a clinical measurement or a replacement for professional support.

share cards

miji generates a daily share card unique to your life: your score over a color gradient that reflects the emotional arc of your day, paired with a single observation line. No two cards look the same because no two days feel the same. You can share your miji card directly with friends or save them privately in-app. miji cards are intentionally abstract so you can share your true feelings without overexposing specifics. Sharing your miji card might serve as a jumping off point to a hard conversation, or to share exciting news. And over time, your miji card archive becomes a visual diary of how your life actually felt to you.

your people

relationship patterns

miji canv track how your emotional baseline shifts in proximity to specific people and interactions. A call with a particular friend might consistently correlate with a score lift, while a recurring meeting might reliably precede a dip. These patterns are surfaced over weeks and months of interactions, not from any single data point. Going through a short rough patch with a loved one won't irreversibly change miji's perception of your relationship with them. The system never labels relationships as good or bad. miji instead presents the data it perceives and lets you draw your own conclusions. miji might get to know you well, but no one can know you better than yourself.

sharing your day

You can share your daily card with anyone else on miji. Your miji card shows your current miji score and a corresponding color gradient, alongside an observation line. No journal content, no raw readings, no specifics are ever included in your miji score card. Recipients can see how your day felt at a glance without getting into the nitty gritty if you don't want to. The miji score card is designed for the kind of check-in that happens between close friends, partners, and family. Sharing is always opt-in, always one card at a time, and you always see exactly what you're sharing.

mutual visibility

When you and another user choose to share your miji scores with one another, miji can surface connection-specific insights between you two. Overlapping patterns begin emerging: days you both felt elevated, or stretches where one person's trajectory diverged from the other's. Both of you have chosen to be transparent, and either can step back at any time, no questions asked. We've designed this feature for those specific relationships where you actually want to know how the other person is doing, and you'd like them to know the same about you.

friend groups

You can create or join small groups of miji users within a shared circle, where everyone's daily cards appear at a glance. our feed shows the day's color and score for each member. Circle insights are determined by identifying overlapping trends your circle's scores. None of your specific session content is ever visible to anyone else. Groups are capped at four users to keep your deepest circle meaningful and intimate.

what stays private

Everything personal to you stays in your hands. Your miji score card lists your composite score, general trendline, daily color, and a short observation tag from miji. No notifications are sent when someone's score shifts unless you've both chosen to do so. No activity feeds, either. Your emotions aren't something to rank against others, and there's no "winning" with miji. The boundary between personal data and shared data is clear, fixed, and controlled entirely by you. Always.

your words

voice-first input

Your journal was built around speaking, but you can type instead if you'd like. When you converse with miji, your words will appear as live transcription. If you misspeak or want to delete something, you can tap any phrase in the transcription to edit, delete, or expand upon. miji's transcription engine runs on-device with no cloud processing of any sensor data. This design choice exists because voice carries emotional data that typing does not, and the perception engine uses that signal to shape what happens next. miji doesn't rewrite or reinterpret anything you've shared, it only processes what you say, surfaces any subtle indicators, and keeps a faithful record for you. Think of miji like a mirror for yourself, not your therapist. miji works best if you continue processing scores with a licensed professional.

contextual prompts

Your journal prompts aren't pulled from a library or from a template. They're generated from what miji's perception engine picks up during the session. Prompts might be generated as a response to miji hearing your tightened voice around a certain topic, a trailing sentence, or a facial expression that miji didn't think quite matched your words. Prompts stay gentle and open-ended, never lead, and never prescribe. But sometiomes life calls for hard and slightly uncomfortable prompts. You never have to answer, but miji works best if you journal with an open mind and heart. miji will follow your lead, whatever you feel comfortable sharing.

what surfaces

At the close of each journal session, the five highest-weighted context words from your session are displayed for you to process. These are the words that miji's perception engine identified as carrying the most emotional significance during your session. Those words are the only directly parsed, journal-derived data that contributes to your score and aggregated profile. They become small markers across weeks and months that trace common themes running through your life.

session history

Free users may access one journal session per day, while premium members can journal as much as they'd like with unlimited sessions, full history, and search capabilities. Past journal entries are stored entirely on-device and can be browsed by date, keywords surfaced, or aggregated session score. Over months of use, your miji journal becomes a searchable emotional record that no other app or diary can replicate.
